FAQ for Teachers

What is Particle Tracks?

Particle Tracks is a web-based educational tool for generating and analyzing particle decay events.
Students use the software to generate random particle decay events. They analyze the events by applying energy and momentum conservation and then use the information to determine the identities of unknown particles.

What background information do my students need to know?

The simulation was originally written for an undergraduate, sophomore-level class in Modern Physics, where it complements material students are learning about energy and momentum in Special Relativity.
Having said this, the simulation is accessible to students in a high school (or first-year university) course in physics. It is assumed that students have been exposed to the concepts of momentum and energy. They should also be familiar with the idea that momentum is a vector, and that momentum conservation implies that each component of the momentum vector is separately conserved. No calculus knowledge is required. No prior exposure to particle physics is assumed.

How much time will it take for my students to do the simulation and analysis?

Depending on the level of preparation of the students, it is reasonable to expect that the entire simulation and analysis can be completed in two to four hours.
It is recommended that teachers go through a few guided examples with their students (see the note about extra resources below), which might take up to an hour. Generating events and performing the online portion of the analysis is relatively quick (under an hour). The offline portion of the analysis, in which energy and momentum conservation are applied to event data, can take another hour or two.

What resources are available to my students?

The demonstration page (link) shows students how to use the website and analyze events. This page includes two brief screencasts that students should be encouraged to watch.
The background page (link) contains more information about particle physics, as well as links to other resources. Much of this information is not necessary for completing the simulation. It is included for students who are interested in learning more about the topic.

What extra resources are available to me as a teacher?

Teachers have access to resources that can be used to teach a brief lesson on energy and momentum conservation in particle physics.
Teachers can also see events that their students have saved, as well as a "grading key" showing the results that the students should get for each of their events.

How do I sign up?

If you're interested in trying out the Particle Tracks simulation with your students, please fill out the following form (link).
An account will be set up for your school, and you will be given "teacher" access to that account so that you can see your students' events. When students log in, they will need to select your school as part of the account creation process.
